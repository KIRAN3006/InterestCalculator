Equated Monthly Installment

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)
is a fixed amount of money that a borrower pays to a lender at
a specified date every month.
It includes both the principal amount and the interest charged on it,
and is calculated based on the loan amount,
the interest rate, and the loan tenure.
The formula to calculate EMI is:

    EMI = P * R *  (1 + R)^n
                 ---------
                (1 + R)^n-1 
        
            Where,
P = Loan amount (principal)
R = Monthly interest rate (annual interest rate divided by 12)
n = Number of months in the loan tenure
