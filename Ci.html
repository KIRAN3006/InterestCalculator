Compound Intrest

Compound interest refers to the interest earned on both the initial
principal amount and the accumulated interest from previous periods. In other words,
the interest earned in each period is added to the principal amount, resulting
in a higher interest earned in the next period.
This creates a compounding effect that can result in significant growth
in the value of an investment over time.
Formula to calculate CI:

    A = P * (1 + R)^n 
                  ---
                  100

    C I = A - P              
        
            Where,
P = Loan amount (principal)
R = Monthly interest rate (annual interest rate divided by 12)
n = Number of months in the loan tenure
A = Total amount
